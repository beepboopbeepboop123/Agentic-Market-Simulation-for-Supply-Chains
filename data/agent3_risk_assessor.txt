### **Risk Assessment for Top 3 Routes**

#### **1. Route: Factory_Shanghai → AirHub_HongKong → AirHub_Frankfurt → AirHub_Chicago → Warehouse_Chicago → Warehouse_NewYork → Customer_NewYork**
- **Congestion Risk:** **High** – Hong Kong and Frankfurt are major hubs; delays likely.
- **Geopolitical Risk:** **Medium** – EU/US routes stable, but potential trade policy shifts.
- **Seasonal Risk:** **Low** – Winter weather minimal in these regions.
- **Capacity Risk:** **High** – Air cargo space may be constrained post-Shanghai closure.
- **Overall Risk:** **High**

#### **2. Route: Factory_Shanghai → Busan_Port → Singapore_Port → Los_Angeles_Port → Warehouse_LA → DC_Atlanta → Customer_NewYork**
- **Congestion Risk:** **Medium** – Busan/Singapore ports are efficient but may face spillover from Shanghai.
- **Geopolitical Risk:** **Low** – US trade routes stable, but US-China tensions could impact.
- **Seasonal Risk:** **Medium** – Winter storms possible in LA/Atlanta.
- **Capacity Risk:** **Medium** – Ports have spare capacity but may see surge in demand.
- **Overall Risk:** **Medium**

#### **3. Route: Factory_Shanghai → AirHub_HongKong → AirHub_Dubai → AirHub_Chicago → Warehouse_Chicago → Warehouse_NewYork → Customer_NewYork**
- **Congestion Risk:** **High** – Dubai is a key hub; delays likely with increased traffic.
- **Geopolitical Risk:** **Medium** – Middle East stability could affect operations.
- **Seasonal Risk:** **Low** – Dubai/Chicago weather is stable in winter.
- **Capacity Risk:** **High** – Air cargo space may be tight.
- **Overall Risk:** **High**

### **SAFEST ROUTE OVERALL: Route 2 (Busan → Singapore → LA → Atlanta)**
- **Why?** Lower geopolitical risk, manageable congestion, and better seasonal resilience compared to air-heavy routes. Ports have spare capacity, reducing bottlenecks.
